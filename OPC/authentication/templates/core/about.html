ü§ñ AI Algorithms Explained

We use several cutting-edge AI algorithms to provide you with the best recommendations. Here's how each one works in simple terms:

üîç TF-IDF (Text Frequency)

What it does: Finds content with similar names or keywords to what you searched for.

How it works: If you search for "Harry Potter," it will recommend other titles that contain similar words like "wizard," "magic," or "fantasy."

Best for: Finding content with similar themes and keywords.

üéØ KNN (K-Nearest Neighbors)

What it does: Finds content that's "nearby" or similar to what you like, just like TF-IDF but smarter.

How it works: It looks at many different features (not just words) to find the closest matches to your preferences.

Best for: Discovering content that shares multiple similarities with your favorites.

üé≠ KNN + Genre

What it does: Uses genres as the main way to match recommendations to your taste.

How it works: If you love action movies, it will prioritize other action films, then look at other features to rank them.

Best for: When you have a strong preference for specific genres.

üß† Neural Networks

What it does: Uses deep learning to understand complex patterns in your preferences.

How it works: Like a digital brain that learns from lots of data to make very sophisticated recommendations.

Best for: Finding hidden gems and surprising recommendations you might love.

üìä Embeddings

What it does: Converts content into mathematical representations to find deep similarities.

How it works: Transforms movies, books, and courses into numbers that capture their essence, making it easier to find truly similar content.

Best for: Finding content that feels similar even if it doesn't share obvious keywords or genres.
